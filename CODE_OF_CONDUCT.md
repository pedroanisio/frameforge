# Code of Conduct

FrameForge contributors are expected to keep project discussions practical,
specific, and respectful.

## Expected Behavior

- Be direct without being hostile.
- Ground technical claims in evidence, reproduction steps, or clear reasoning.
- Assume maintainers and contributors are acting in good faith unless there is
  concrete evidence otherwise.
- Keep issue and pull-request threads focused on the work being discussed.
- Credit prior work and disclose relevant provenance for contributed examples,
  fixtures, fonts, media, or datasets.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Deliberately derailing technical threads.
- Submitting code, data, fixtures, or media you do not have the right to share.
- Coordinated abuse of issue trackers, discussions, or review queues.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues or pull requests;
or limit participation when behavior damages the project or its contributors.

To report conduct concerns, contact the maintainer listed in `pyproject.toml`.
