# FrameGraph — Market Estimates v3

*Successor to v2 — standalone. Adds the marketing-agency / creative-automation segment, and tightens scope (what FrameGraph is, and what it deliberately is not). Figures are third-party analyst estimates and ranges, shown for scale — not a revenue forecast or financial advice.*

---

## The thesis, in one line

> FrameGraph is a **layer — produce, convert, derive.** It wins by sitting *under* the apps and stores — powering agencies' production, sitting on top of the knowledge base, handing off to Word and PowerPoint — and it loses the moment it tries to *become* them.

Every scope question ("could it be the knowledge base? replace Office? be a creative tool?") resolves the same way: **integrate with the incumbent, don't impersonate it.**

## What changed since v2

1. **Added a market + a beachhead:** creative automation / creative management platforms (CMP), where marketing agencies and brand teams live.
2. **Tightened scope (new section):** FrameGraph is the **publishing/derivation layer *over*** a corporate knowledge base — not the knowledge base — and it **replaces the *production labor* of Word/PowerPoint**, not the editor (a stated non-goal). These are guardrails that protect the pitch from category-error objections.

---

## The reframe (the category)

FrameGraph is the horizontal layer across the **whole life of a visual document — produce, convert (any→any), retarget (one source → every surface), and derive (accessibility, data, search)** — riding the generative-AI wave. It sits under design tools, slide tools, document systems, dashboards, CMS/DAM, publishing, accessibility, and creative ops — all separate only because a human operated each.

## The beachhead — four wedges

1. **Accessibility-by-construction document generation.** Regulated and deadline-driven (EAA in force; thousands of US ADA suits/yr). Incumbents *remediate after the fact, by hand*; FrameGraph emits tagged PDF/UA from the model **for free**. Urgency + structural edge = strongest new wedge.
2. **Single-source omnichannel.** One source → SEC filing + investor deck + Instagram story, each retargeted to its surface, **provably consistent** — a figure that changes once propagates everywhere.
3. **Marketing agencies / creative automation** ⟵ new. The exact CMP job — one creative → every placement, on-brand, fast (DCO / versioning / resizing / localization), where agencies are ~37% of buyers. **Sell it as the production engine *under* agencies and brand teams (white-label / API) — a margin multiplier, not a competitor to their creative.** Agencies bill for *production* (the disruptable part) and sell *taste* (the part FrameGraph never touches).
4. **Branded documents & dynamic images at scale + a verified-output API** for AI products (the original wedge — still valid).

## Scope discipline — what FrameGraph is *not* (and the sharper version of each)

| Tempting expansion | Verdict | The defensible version instead |
|---|---|---|
| **The corporate knowledge base** (Notion / Confluence / SharePoint / Glean / vector DB) | **No** — wrong category. FrameGraph stores a *document's* model, not what the company *knows*; no edge in storage/search vs. entrenched incumbents. | **The publishing/derivation layer *over* it.** Ingest from the sources of truth (warehouse, CMS, DAM, wiki); derive every document, deck, filing, and post; **guarantee every surface agrees** ("the SEC table and the Instagram story can never disagree"). The KB holds the facts; FrameGraph holds the rendering contract. Be the *canonical renderer / system of record for published artifacts.* |
| **A Word / PowerPoint replacement (the editor / app)** | **No** — and a **stated non-goal** (the output-space record lists "a WYSIWYG editor" as out, by decision). A losing war vs. Microsoft/Google's installed base, collaboration, comments, track-changes, lock-in. Pitching an "Office killer" is a category error an investor catches in one question. | **Replace the *production labor*, not the editor.** FrameGraph already *emits* DOCX/PPTX, so for the large share of Office documents *produced from data or intent* (reports, statements, board packs, recurring decks, filings, one-pagers) the human-operating-the-app step disappears — the machine generates the file, on-brand, checked, accessible, and **hands back an editable .docx/.pptx** the human can still tweak. Complement the apps; disrupt the production tail. |
| **A CMS / DAM / CMP app or store** | **No** — that's the store/workflow layer. | **Integrate with and power them** — be the generation-and-derivation engine underneath, via connectors and API. |
