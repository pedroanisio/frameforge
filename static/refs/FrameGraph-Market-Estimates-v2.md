# FrameGraph — Market Estimates v2

*Updated after the output-space record + V2 roadmap. The product is broader than "produce business documents," so the sizing moves up and the framing changes. Figures are third-party analyst estimates and ranges, shown for scale — not a revenue forecast or financial advice.*

---

## What changed (why the number moves)

The three documents reveal FrameGraph is not a generator — it's a **verifiable IR for visual documents with an any→any hub**, plus a **single-source → every-surface** engine that also **derives accessibility and data**. That adds three markets the old slide didn't count:

1. **The hub — "a Pandoc for visual documents."** Import (PDF/image → FG) × export (PDF, DOCX, PPTX, EPUB, InDesign, LaTeX, SVG, HTML…) makes every importer × exporter pair reachable. That's document **conversion / interchange / publishing**, not just authoring.
2. **One source → every audience (2.3 → 3.0).** A content/presentation split that retargets one source to every surface (print, screen, social) and derives each audience's artifact by select+filter — the SEC filing and the Instagram story *can never disagree, by construction*. That's **omnichannel content operations** (CMS / DAM / single-source publishing) — and a consistency/compliance wedge.
3. **Accessibility & semantics by construction.** Tagged PDF/UA, ARIA tree, reading-order, Braille, SSML — *derived from the model*, not remediated after the fact. That's the **digital-accessibility** market, with a hard regulatory tailwind — and a structural advantage over remediation incumbents.

## The reframe (one line)

> FrameGraph is the horizontal layer across the **whole life of a visual document — produce, convert (any→any), retarget (one source → every surface), and derive (accessibility, data, search)** — riding the generative-AI wave.

It isn't entering one market; it sits under design tools, slide tools, document systems, dashboards, CMS/DAM, publishing, and accessibility — all of which were separate only because a human operated each.

## The sharpened beachhead — three wedges

1. **Accessibility-by-construction document generation.** Regulated and deadline-driven (European Accessibility Act in force; thousands of US ADA suits/yr). Incumbents *remediate after the fact, by hand*; FrameGraph emits tagged PDF/UA from the model **for free**. Urgency + structural edge = the strongest new wedge.
2. **Single-source omnichannel.** One source → SEC filing + investor deck + Instagram story, each retargeted to its surface, **provably consistent** — a figure that changes once propagates everywhere. Consistency/compliance for regulated reporting *and* brand for marketing ops.
3. **Branded documents & dynamic images at scale + a verified-output API** for AI products (the original wedge — still valid).
